# Authorship And Specificity

Use this route when a result feels generated, cheap, interchangeable, over-templated, or visually busy without being designed. It is a causality test, not a blacklist of fashionable styles.

## Diagnose The Real Failure

Generated-looking work usually lacks a product-caused decision:

- the same centered or stacked skeleton survives after every noun and logo change;
- cards, chips, badges, gradients, blur, giant type, or terminal chrome appear because the category suggested them, not because the task requires them;
- fake charts, metrics, testimonials, logos, dashboards, terminals, or screenshots counterfeit proof;
- dead controls imply functionality the artifact does not have;
- every section repeats the same container, spacing, hierarchy, and reveal;
- component polish is high while the primary artifact remains subordinate;
- motion decorates arrival but explains no state, relationship, or continuity;
- a signature move can be removed without changing comprehension, workflow, evidence, or character.

Do not “fix” this by auto-selecting monochrome brutalism, beige luxury, acid grids, oversized serif italics, chrome, or hostile motion. A predictable rebellion is still predictable.

## Run The Causality Test

For each major decision, complete:

~~~text
decision:
caused by: product | data | workflow | audience | material | place | proof | interaction
visible consequence:
what a competitor could not reuse:
state where it earns its cost:
kill condition:
~~~

If the cause is only a style adjective, the decision is costume. If a competitor can transplant the whole surface by replacing nouns, the composition is not authored yet.

At least one major decision must come from the actual artifact, workflow, data shape, or proof object. Brand personality may sharpen that decision; it may not replace it.

## Require Real Specificity

Use:

- real product objects and domain language;
- plausible data shapes with explicit fixture provenance;
- verified screenshots, media, outputs, places, or materials;
- real action verbs and consequences;
- the actual constraints of frequency, pressure, input, aspect ratio, and state;
- existing brand or product assets when they earned continuity.

When evidence is missing, show the gap. Do not invent customers, awards, metrics, terminal output, maps, charts, or product behavior to make the proposal look complete.

An illustrative mock may use clearly labeled synthetic data when the design decision depends on shape rather than truth. Mark it synthetic, make the distribution realistic enough to stress the layout, and never present it as product proof.

## Author A Component Ecology

Avoid the page-builder reflex where everything becomes the same rounded card. Define a small ecology by role:

- frame or field;
- primary artifact;
- navigation or mode switch;
- control cluster;
- evidence or detail region;
- status and feedback;
- interruption or decision point.

Components may share tokens without sharing identical containment. Use borders, rules, spacing, background changes, typography, position, or direct adjacency when a card adds no meaning.

Repeated components need coherent geometry and state behavior. Variety is not permission for random radii, shadows, icon families, paddings, or motion. The system should feel authored; the composition should not feel cloned.

## Spend Detail Where It Changes Belief

High-value craft includes:

- a product-specific information shape;
- treated real screenshots or artifacts;
- coherent icons and vectors at actual sizes;
- deliberate typography and line breaks;
- responsive crops and focal points;
- complete pending, error, permission, empty, and recovery states;
- motion tied to continuity or feedback;
- careful edge, alignment, scrollbar, and safe-area finish.

Low-value theater includes:

- decorative dashboard div art;
- impossible glass over unreadable content;
- generic particles, noise, grid overlays, or glowing orbs;
- random 3D tilt and cursor followers;
- animated counters without trusted values;
- fake browser chrome or terminal text;
- dozens of hover effects on controls used every minute.

Detail earns cost when it improves task speed, trust, comprehension, orientation, or memorable product character.

## Non-Website Surfaces

Do not force complex interfaces into landing-page grammar.

### HUD or game interface

The play field, mechanic, glance distance, threat, safe area, and input own the composition. Navigation bars, stacked sections, and marketing-card rhythm usually do not.

### Command center

Urgency, freshness, ownership, evidence, and next action own the hierarchy. A wallboard and an operator console are different modes, not responsive variants of a generic dashboard.

### Studio or editor

The artifact and direct manipulation own the frame. Toolbars and inspectors are supporting instruments, not equal feature panels.

### Spatial, immersive, or installation UI

Viewing distance, environment, latency, orientation, occlusion, hardware input, and recovery define the system. A 1440-pixel browser mock does not prove it.

### Dense data workspace

Comparison, selection, provenance, confidence, and decision consequences own the structure. Decorative whitespace cannot replace scan clarity.

## Cheap-Generation Gate

Before proof, answer:

- What real constraint caused the composition?
- What is the primary artifact, and does it visibly dominate?
- Which category default and second reflex were killed?
- Which common pattern stayed, and why is it the clearest grammar?
- What content or evidence is real, synthetic, missing, or blocked?
- Which controls are truly interactive?
- What is the signature move, and what task or meaning does it serve?
- Does rhythm express hierarchy, or repeat one gap everywhere?
- Does motion explain state or continuity, or merely announce rendering?
- Can the surface survive long content, error, narrow width, and repeated use?
- Could a competitor reuse it after a noun swap?

Fail when the defense is a list of fonts, colors, shadows, gradients, effects, or anti-pattern bans. Pass when the product causality remains visible without the design-process explanation.

## Replacement Language

Do not say “make it less generic.” Say:

~~~text
generic structure:
why it could belong to anyone:
real product cause:
replacement composition:
content/assets required:
signature behavior:
states that must survive:
proof target:
~~~

Specificity is an implementation contract, not a compliment.
