# Marketing Pages

Use this for landing pages, pricing pages, launch pages, portfolio/service pages, and hybrid surfaces where the design must persuade. Do not apply these defaults to product apps, dashboards, forms, admin screens, or high-frequency workflows.

Keep this route focused on implementation and review decisions that affect conversion clarity, proof, and trust.

## Preflight

Before changing layout or visuals, identify:

- Offer: what is being sold, launched, explained, or requested.
- Audience: buyer, evaluator, developer, designer, recruiter, operator, consumer, or mixed.
- Conversion: signup, contact, purchase, upgrade, demo, download, subscribe, or trust-building.
- Proof: product screenshots, customer proof, metrics, logos, testimonials, security/compliance, examples, or real output.
- Objections: price, complexity, trust, migration, lock-in, time-to-value, risk, maintenance, or support.
- Constraints: SEO metadata, analytics events, legal copy, form names/order, existing IA, brand tokens, and available assets.

Done when page structure follows the offer and objections, not a stock section order.

## Landing Page Route

Minimum useful structure:

- First viewport: specific H1, short support copy, primary CTA, one credibility signal, and a visible hint of the next proof/product section.
- Proof or product artifact early: real screenshot, workflow state, demo frame, output sample, customer evidence, or explicit asset gap.
- Argument sections: each section does a different job such as pain, mechanism, workflow, comparison, outcome, proof, or FAQ.
- CTA rhythm: primary CTA appears where intent naturally peaks; secondary actions are not visually louder.
- Bottom: objection handling, final CTA, and trust/legal details when relevant.

Avoid repeating the same three-card feature block. Use different section roles: proof block, workflow strip, comparison surface, media moment, testimonial, pricing, FAQ, or docs handoff.

## Pricing Page Route

Before designing cards, lock:

- Plan model: per-seat, usage, tiered, flat, freemium, enterprise/contact, or custom.
- Comparison basis: features, limits, users, volume, support, compliance, or outcome.
- Recommended plan rule: why it is recommended and for whom.
- Risk reversal: trial, cancellation, support, migration, SLA, security, or refund.
- Edge cases: annual/monthly toggle, taxes, currency, enterprise, overages, usage slider, custom quote, discounts, and hidden fees.

Pricing UI should make comparison fast:

- Align plan names, prices, periods, limits, CTAs, and feature rows.
- Use tabular numbers for dynamic prices.
- Keep the recommended plan visually distinct but not deceptive.
- Use concise tooltips or expandable detail for complex limits.
- Keep FAQ and objections near the decision point.

## Visual Direction

Marketing pages can be expressive, but expression must carry the offer:

- Derive palette, imagery, and signature detail from the product, audience, brand asset, real scene, or reference.
- Use one memorable signature move, not a stack of decorative effects.
- Let real media carry product/place/object pages; avoid fake dashboards and placeholder metadata.
- Use motion for attention sequence, product comprehension, or delight at low frequency.

Read `reference-capture.md` when a URL, video, image, or inspiration would materially improve fidelity. Read `visual-recipes.md` only after the page job is clear.

## Review Checks

- The page answers "what is it, who is it for, why trust it, what happens next?"
- First viewport fits small laptop and mobile without headline overwrap, CTA loss, or label clutter.
- Each section has a distinct persuasive role.
- Pricing cards compare the same kind of information in the same place.
- Real proof appears before decoration.
- SEO, analytics hooks, form behavior, accessibility, and legal copy are preserved unless explicitly changed.
