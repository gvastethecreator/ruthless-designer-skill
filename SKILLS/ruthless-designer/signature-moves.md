# Signature Moves

Use this when the surface needs to be memorable, product-specific, or clearly better than generic public design patterns.

A signature move is visible and useful. It makes the task clearer, the proof stronger, the artifact more inspectable, the state more trustworthy, or the brand more specific. It is not decoration.

## Selection Rule

Pick one primary move and, at most, one supporting move.

Choose a move that can answer:

- What product, object, workflow, dataset, place, material, or audience forced this?
- Where does the user see it in the first meaningful screen?
- What does it help them understand, compare, trust, or do?
- How will desktop and mobile proof show it survived?

Kill the move when:

- It only makes the page look expensive.
- It hides the main task.
- It needs fake screenshots or invented metrics.
- It cannot survive long text, empty/error states, focus, reduced motion, or narrow viewports.
- It is the same move the category would produce by reflex.

## Product Moves

- Current-object dominance: give the active canvas, record, map, media, document, schedule, or case most of the visual weight.
- Inspector tether: controls visually point to the object or region they change.
- Decision lens: organize the layout around the user's comparison or approval decision, not around feature categories.
- Risk row grammar: encode freshness, confidence, severity, or ownership into the list/table structure.
- State-first card: make empty, loading, error, permission, and recovery states as designed as the happy path.
- Timeline as control: let history, stages, or upcoming work act as navigation rather than passive metadata.
- Recovery rail: expose undo, retry, restore, and safe-fallback actions in one consistent place.
- Density gradient: let high-frequency areas become dense while rare actions get more air.
- Command surface: make shortcuts, quick actions, and command search the visible productivity layer.
- Live preview contract: every setting change has immediate, reversible visible feedback.
- One status model: collapse scattered badges into one readable status object.
- Evidence pane: selected item always has source, proof, and next action nearby.

## Brand Moves

- Artifact-first hero: real product, object, place, screenshot, generated bitmap, or output sample carries the first impression.
- Physical palette source: colors come from a real material, image, scene, place, product, or brand artifact.
- Proof before poetry: a metric, case, screenshot, quote, comparison, or sample output appears before decorative narrative.
- Memorable constraint: one hard rule shapes the page, such as monochrome plus one material color, annotated crops, visible grid, object-scale typography, or image-led sections.
- Asymmetric section roles: each fold has a different job instead of repeating equal card grids.
- Public claim / private proof: bold claim paired with a precise proof object, not vague support copy.
- Real media edge: images, video, maps, screenshots, or product media get deliberate crop, outline, caption, and inspection affordance.
- Objection as section: price, trust, migration, time-to-value, risk, support, or lock-in becomes a designed moment.
- Type voice axis: typography expresses audience and offer rather than category fashion.
- Conversion hinge: the CTA appears where the proof peaks, not only in hero and footer.

## Hybrid Moves

- Product mechanism hero: the first viewport shows how the product works, not only what it promises.
- Brand-to-product handoff: expressive top section snaps into restrained product controls at a clear boundary.
- Sample-to-real transition: sample data is visibly replaced by the user's first real object during onboarding.
- Pricing as fit finder: plans are organized by user situation and limits, not just columns of features.
- Docs proof path: quickstart, code, live result, and troubleshooting form one visible task path.
- Trust ladder: claims escalate from surface proof to security/compliance to support/recovery.
- Before/after machine: manual/current state and automated/target state share a single comparison frame.

## Motion Moves

- Origin-aware reveal: anchored surfaces open from the trigger or object they belong to.
- State continuity: selected tabs, cards, previews, or media preserve object identity across views.
- Commit feedback: save, publish, approve, delete, or export has a precise confirmation motion.
- Progress with consequence: loading and processing show what is happening and what happens next.
- Gesture affordance: drag, swipe, reorder, or resize feels physically bounded and reversible.
- Reduced-motion identity: the design still has a signature without travel-heavy animation.

## Data And Proof Moves

- Annotated evidence: screenshots, metrics, samples, or outputs include labels that explain what to inspect.
- Confidence map: uncertainty, freshness, or source quality becomes a visual layer.
- Comparison ruler: differences are aligned to a shared axis, timeline, price row, quality band, or risk scale.
- Real sample library: content examples are domain-specific and varied, not placeholder names.
- Failure specimen: show a real error/recovery example as proof of product maturity.
- Source trace: important claims point to a source, timestamp, file, customer, route, or artifact.

## Direction Note

Record the chosen move like this:

```text
Signature move:
- move:
- why this product needs it:
- where it appears:
- state coverage:
- proof target:
- kill condition:
```

Completion criterion: if the move is removed, the design becomes less specific, less useful, or less trustworthy. If removing it only makes the page simpler, it was decoration.
