# Direction Sprint

Use this when the brief is open-ended, high-ambition, "incredible", "standout", "not generic", brand-heavy, portfolio-like, or blank-canvas enough that the first good idea may still be a category reflex.

The direction sprint prevents competent sameness. It forces incompatible options before the system is built.

## When To Sprint

Sprint when any of these are true:

- The user asks for a new visual direction, redesign, landing page, portfolio, hero, prototype, or product surface from little context.
- The user says premium, beautiful, brutal, standout, unforgettable, not AI-looking, or not generic.
- The current artifact is competent but could fit a competitor after a logo swap.
- The first idea is a centered hero, three cards, a purple gradient, terminal cosplay, beige luxury, editorial monochrome, decorative glass, or fake dashboard preview.

Skip only for narrow production bugs, tiny component fixes, strict design-system compliance, or accessibility/performance repairs where the direction already exists.

## Sprint Order

Produce three incompatible direction cards. Keep them brief enough to act on.

```text
Direction:
Read:
Core task:
Composition:
Signature move:
Palette source:
Type/voice:
Motion grammar:
State/proof hook:
What this kills:
Primary risk:
Proof target:
```

Use these slots:

- Direction A: the competent category default. Name it so it can be killed or constrained.
- Direction B: the strongest product-specific direction. It should make the task, data, artifact, or workflow shape the screen.
- Direction C: the sharper brand or experience direction. It should have more variance, but still survive accessibility and responsive proof.

Do not build all three. Pick one, or merge only when the merge makes the hierarchy sharper rather than safer.

## Selection Rule

Choose the direction that best satisfies all four:

- It makes the main task faster to understand.
- It gives the surface a reason to look this way.
- It can be proven in desktop and mobile/narrow evidence.
- It has one visible signature move that is not decorative filler.

Reject the direction when:

- The signature move is only an effect.
- The direction needs fake assets to make sense.
- It hides the product behind brand theater.
- It would collapse under long text, empty/error states, keyboard focus, or mobile.
- A competitor could use it by swapping nouns and logo.

## Direction Reset

If two implementation passes are `flat` or `worse`, do not polish.

```text
Direction failed because:
Kill:
Keep:
New direction:
New signature move:
Proof target:
```

Then rebuild from the new target. A failed direction is information, not a reason to sand the same weak idea.

## Output

For open-ended work, keep a compact record in the design ledger:

```text
Sprint:
- killed default:
- killed second reflex:
- selected direction:
- signature move:
- proof target:
```

Completion criterion: the chosen direction can be explained in one sentence without aesthetic buzzwords, and the rendered artifact makes that sentence visible.
